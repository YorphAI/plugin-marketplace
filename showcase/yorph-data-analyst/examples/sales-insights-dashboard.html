Key Findings

1. November is your super-month. Nov 2003 ($1.03M) and Nov 2004 ($1.09M) each generated ~2x the revenue of any other month. This extreme Q4 seasonality is your most important revenue pattern.

2. Classic Cars dominates at 37.5% of revenue — more than double the next line (Vintage Cars at 18.5%). Together they account for 56% of all revenue.

3. EMEA is your largest region at 49.6%. North America follows at 26.8%. Asia Pacific and Japan together are under 10%.

4. Heavy customer concentration. Your top 2 customers (Euro Shopping Channel + Mini Gifts) generate 15.6% of revenue. Top 20 customers = 43.4%.

5. Revenue grew strongly year-over-year. 2003: $3.5M → 2004: $4.7M (+34%). 2005 was on pace for ~$4.3M annualized through May.

Trust Notes

Data cleaning applied: Status labels were standardized from 22 variants to 7 categories (e.g., "Shipped"/"shipped"/"SHIPPED" → "Shipped"). Deal sizes were standardized from 13 variants to 3 (Small, Medium, Large). Territory was standardized from 12 variants to 4 regions. Country names were standardized from 44 variants to ~25 (e.g., "US"/"USA"/"United States" → "United States").

Missing data: ~7.2% of deal sizes and ~3.7% of product lines were blank and labeled "Unknown." ~5% of quarter IDs were missing and were derived from order dates.

Assumptions: All order statuses (including Disputed, On Hold, Cancelled) are included in revenue totals. If you want to exclude non-fulfilled orders, the numbers would shift — cancelled/hold/disputed/in-process orders account for ~6% of total revenue.

Region gaps: ~14% of revenue ($1.4M) comes from customers with no territory assigned in the source data, shown as "Unknown" in the region breakdown.
