How it works
Three layers. Linear cost.
The expensive part — semantic understanding — runs once per unique value, not once per pair. Everything after is plain code.
Layer 01
Text Normalization

Abbreviation expansion, casing normalization, and domain-specific shorthand resolution. "DRTIOS NCH" becomes "doritos nacho cheese" before any similarity scoring begins.

O(n) · regex + rules
Layer 02
TF-IDF Similarity

Two parallel vectorizers — word n-grams (1–3) and character n-grams (3–5). Word-level handles abbreviations; character-level catches misspellings and truncations. Combined cosine similarity surfaces the top-5 candidates per entry.

O(n) · no pair-wise comparison
Layer 03
Heuristic Re-ranking

Brand signals, size tokens, and flavor variant keywords boost candidate scores. Confidence gaps determine auto-accept vs. low-confidence flagging for human review. No guessing silently passed off as fact.

O(n) · deterministic scoring
What it had to solve
Five deliberate traps. All planted on purpose.
The dataset was synthetically generated with known challenges to stress-test the join pipeline.
Challenge 1 · ~75% of rows
Abbreviation & shorthand

Reps used every abbreviation imaginable — brand initials, size codes, product shorthand. No two styles consistent.

"HN Cheerios 18oz" → Cheerios Honey Nut 18.8oz
"Tylenol ES 100ct" → Tylenol Extra Strength 500mg 100ct
Challenge 2 · ~40% of rows
Brand name variations

Brand names appeared abbreviated, misspelled, or omitted entirely. "kelloggs" vs "Kellanova", "P&G" products with no brand mention.

"cliff bar choc chip 6pk" → Clif Bar Chocolate Chip 6ct
"smuckers straw jam 18oz" → Smucker's Strawberry Jam 18oz
Challenge 3 · ~6% of rows
Extreme mangling

Dropped vowels, all-caps garbling, and phonetic guesses that break even fuzzy string matchers.

"DRTIOS NCH 9OZ" → Doritos Nacho Cheese 9.25oz
"ORIO 14.3OZ" → Oreo Original Cookies 14.3oz
Challenge 4 · ~3% of rows
Genuine ambiguity

Some entries are legitimately unresolvable without external context — correctly flagged as low-confidence rather than silently guessed.

"diet cola 12pk" → Diet Coke or Diet Pepsi?
"cola 12 pack" → flagged · needs human review
Challenge 5 · ~20% of rows
Missing size / format

Entries omit the size entirely, using descriptive words instead. Requires understanding that "small can" and "lg box" map to specific SKUs.

"Red Bull small can" → Red Bull Energy Drink 8.4oz
"frosted flakes lg box" → Frosted Flakes 24oz
Confidence breakdown
Honest scoring, not just a match

Every resolution includes a confidence score and a runner-up candidate. Low-confidence rows are surfaced for review — not silently wrong.
